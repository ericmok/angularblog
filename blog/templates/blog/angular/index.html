Long-Form Posts

Don't have to be just a wall of text!

A site for rigorous internet commentary:

Be Rigorous

Mark logical fallacies on sentences

Not Implemented
Choose from a list of fallacies and tag it onto groups of sentences. Sentences are grouped into arguments that are filed into a database for entailment analysis. When users make a new post, they can refer to previous arguments made by other users.

Attack No Strawmen

Comment on individual sentences of a post

Under Development
A wall of text can have so many pieces, it can be hard to analyze rigorously. Users can comment, score, and tag individual sentences instead of the entire post. As users create new long-form posts, they can see previously tagged sentences for reference.

Re-Revise Mistakes

Track replies to your post even if you edit

No need for "EDIT" statements. User replies are made on a per-sentence basis. As you edit your post, these replies will transfer over so you can still keep those old replies.

Why?

Because 140 characters are not enough

Because the internet makes direct democracy plausible

The internet lowers the barrier of entry for people who have an opinion to have a public voice. Unfortunately, producing rigorous discussion on the internet has proven to be notoriously hard. A structured environment can enable collaborative criticism, and help people to form high-quality opinions.

This website is a toy to show how long form commenting might be possible.
