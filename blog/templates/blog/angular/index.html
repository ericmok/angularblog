A wall of text can have so many pieces, it can be hard to analyze rigorously. Users can comment, score, and tag individual sentences instead of the entire post.

Re-Revise Mistakes

Track replies to your post even if you edit

No need for "EDIT" statements. User replies are made on a per-sentence basis. As you edit your post, these replies will transfer over so you can still keep those old replies.

Why?

The internet can make direct democracy possible by enabling collaborative criticism and helping people form high-quality opinions.

It lowers the barrier of entry for people who have an opinion to have a public voice. Unfortunately, producing rigorous discussion on the internet is notoriously hard. The larger a wall of text is, the less users will engage in thorough criticism.

This website is a toy to show how long form commenting might be possible. Users can comment on individual sentences as well as mark sets of sentences as containing a logical fallacy. When crafting a new post, users can look up sentences that were used in the past, and use them as accepted premises so they can bootstrap their argument without having to explain them.
