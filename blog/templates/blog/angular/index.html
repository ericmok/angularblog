Long-Form Posts

Don't have to be just a wall of text!

This is a return to rigorous internet commentary

Be Rigorous

Mark logical fallacies on sentences

This feature is pending implementation.
Choose from a list of fallacies and tag it onto groups of sentences. An argument may consist of a set of sentences. As users tag arguments, the arguments will be filed into a database for entailment analysis. When users make a new post, they can refer to tried and tested arguments of the past.

Attack No Strawmen

Comment on individual sentences of a post

This is currently under development.
A wall of text can have so many pieces, it can be hard to analyze rigorously. Users can comment on individual sentences instead of the entire post. Each sentence can be scored and tagged. As users create new long-form posts, they can refer to previously used sentences to make their point with less fear of repeating someone else's errors.

Re-Revise Mistakes

Track replies to your post even if you edit

No more "EDIT" statements. User replies are made on a per-sentence basis. As you edit your post, these replies will transfer over so you can still keep those old replies.

Why?

Because 140 characters are not enough

Because the internet makes direct democracy plausible and possibly productive

The internet lowers the barrier of entry for people who have an opinion to have a public voice. Unfortunately, rigorous discussion on the internet has proven to be very hard.

The internet is notorious for how counter-productive it can be.

YouTube comments have shown that meaningful commentary on the internet is actually an accomplishment. Reddit's witch hunt has shown that productive collaboration on the internet with random strangers can fail even when people are well intentioned. There is more to meaningful contribution than participation. There needs to be collective rigorous analysis.

When President Obama wrote on Reddit, technical discussions were not pursued in favor of short-form rhetorical posts. Rhetoric decreased the signal-to-noise ratio of his posts, making them less productive in the pursuit of correctness.

The internet can empower collaborative criticism and debate and help people to form high-quality opinions. But it is not enough to be rigorous. A place of learning should be a safe place for people to make mistakes so they can be motivated to partake in the collective pursuit of truth.

This website is a toy to show how long form commenting might be possible.
